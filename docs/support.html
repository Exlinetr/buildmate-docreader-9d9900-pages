Frequently Asked Questions

How does DocReader work?
DocReader allows you to convert images into PDF documents. You can capture new photos using your device camera or select existing images from your gallery, then compile them into a high-quality PDF file.
Are my photos uploaded to any servers?
No. Your privacy is paramount. DocReader processes all conversions locally on your mobile device. None of your photos, camera feeds, or generated PDF documents are ever uploaded to the cloud or external servers.
Can I convert multiple images at once?
Yes. DocReader supports multi-page PDF generation, allowing you to select and compile multiple images into a single, combined PDF document in one go.
What image formats are supported?
DocReader supports standard mobile image formats, including JPEG, PNG, and HEIC, which are automatically converted and optimized into a single PDF document.
Where are my converted PDF files saved?
Your generated PDF files are saved directly to your device's local storage. You can access, share, or export them to other applications using your device's native sharing menu.
Is an internet connection required?
No internet connection is required to use DocReader. Since all processing is performed locally on-device, you can convert your images to PDF anytime and anywhere, even when offline.

Troubleshooting

DocReader cannot access my photos or camera
Please open your device settings, find DocReader in your app list, and verify that both Camera and Photo Library permissions are enabled.
PDF generation fails or is too slow
If you are combining a very large number of high-resolution images, the process may run out of memory. Try compiling fewer images at a time or closing other active applications.
Images appear blurry in the final PDF
Ensure that your photos are captured under good lighting conditions and that the document text is clearly in focus before finalizing the conversion.
Cannot find my saved PDF documents
When you generate a PDF, use the export option to save the file to your device's default files app or cloud folder of choice.
The app is frozen or crashed
Force close DocReader, make sure your operating system is updated to the latest version, and try launching the app again.
